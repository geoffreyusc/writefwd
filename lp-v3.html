// the problem

You don't have a writing problem. You have an editing problem.

The internal critic fires before the first sentence is finished. You write it, hate it, delete it. You rewrite it. You hate that too. Nothing exists. The document is still blank.

The editor is in the room. writefwd removes it.

"I focus on saying the thing rather than how I'm saying the thing. I can't hate a sentence that does its job."
—the newsletter you've rewritten the opening of eleven times
—the email addressed to "an audience" which is nobody
—the chapter you've been warming up for long enough
—the thing sitting in drafts for two weeks that's almost kinda done
—the hard conversation you keep writing and deleting instead of sending

You already know freewriting works. You just can't stop yourself from editing. writefwd enforces the discipline you already believe in.
